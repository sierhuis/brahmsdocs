Welcome to Ejenta

The Ejenta team develops and maintains Brahms, an intelligent multi-agent environment used for developing models of work practice. These models can be used for simulating and analyzing current and future work practices in an organization, or for providing agent-based software solutions to support the people that are part of the modeled work practice.

Brahms is a data-driven (forward chaining) multi-agent discrete event environment usable for both complex simulations and for developing agentic software solutions requiring the use of multiple intelligent agents.

Brahms - Modeling and Simulating Work Practice

Dr. Maarten Sierhuis has published the definitive book on Brahms. In this book, he discusses the Brahms language and its application in modeling and simulating complex work practices. A full PDF version is available.

Brahms in Action

Brahms is one of the key technologies used to improve the performance and productivity of human-computer systems on NASA missions. It has been instrumental in human-robot interaction projects, including the Mobile Agents Project and field tests at the Mars Desert Research Station.

Documentation

Papers

This page lists all the papers that have been published about Brahms and related projects. Some papers are available for download in PDF format, some are available in HTML format and some are only available as a reference.

Sierhuis, M. (2001). Modeling and Simulating Work Practice. BRAHMS: a multiagent modeling and simulation language for work system analysis and design, Ph.D. thesis, Social Science and Informatics (SWI), University of Amsterdam, SIKS Dissertation Series No. 2001-10, Amsterdam, The Netherlands, ISBN 90-6464-849-2.

Clancey, W. J., Sierhuis, M., Damer, B., and Brodsky, B. (2001). The Brahms-FMARS Project: A New Perspective on Simulating Human-Robotic Mars Missions, DigitalSpace Corporation.

Sierhuis, M., Clancey, W. J., and van Hoof, R. (2003). Brahms: A Multiagent Modeling Environment for Simulating Work Practice in Organizations, In the proceedings of the second international joint conference on Autonomous Agents and Multiagent Systems (AAMAS 2003), July 14-18, 2003, Melbourne, Australia.

Clancey, W. J., Sierhuis, M., Kaskiris, C., and van Hoof, R. (2004). Advantages of Brahms for Specifying and Implementing a Multiagent Human-Robotic Exploration System, In the proceedings of the third international joint conference on Autonomous Agents and Multiagent Systems (AAMAS 2004), July 19-23, 2004, New York, NY.

Sierhuis, M., Clancey, W. J., Seah, C., and van Hoof, R. (2005). Brahms: An Agent-based Modeling Environment for Simulating Human-Robot Interactions in a Human-centered Design Process, In the proceedings of the first international conference on Space Mission Challenges for Information Technology (SMC-IT 2003), July 13-15, 2003, Pasadena, CA.
